Duo Basinga

It's when she came back from her first visit to Kinshasa, D.R.C. – her country of origin – that Tatiana-Mosio BONGONGA suggested her father to create with him a routine for the Circus of Tomorrow World Festival (“Festival International du Cirque de Demain”) in January 2012 .

For her and her father, the competition came as a pretext. A way to explore what ties them together, connects them. To question their relationship, their origins and the crossing of their personal stories.

The jury awarded them the Gold Medal, and two prices : the Britain Circus award and the price for the best Costume.

Basinga is a word in Lingala meaning strings, links.

Links between a father and a daughter. Links between two artists who, unconsciously, have both chosen to express themselves through vibrating strings. She is a rope dancer, high wire walker
He is a guitar player and singer.
Together they are this touching high-flying duet. The Basinga duo.

This duet - created as an act in 2012 - will be rehearsed again and expanded in 2015 to become a thirty-minutes show, ready to be performed in nearly any conditions, exploring even further those links that brings us together.

Tangente

Intended to be created at the summer 2016, Tangent (working title) will be an open-air, participatory high-wire show for outdoors or enclosed spaces of big volume.

The high-wire walker rises in music, over the audience, on ropes that appear and vanish mysteriously. Spectators unite to ease and help the journey of the performer who, the higher she goes, seems to become a more and more tenuous, calm and uncluttered character.
She molts. Mutates.
Costumes keep changing. Songs arise from nowhere.
The more the performance becomes dramatical, the more the feat of one person fades behind a collective emotion, the more the border between artists and audience wears away.

Moving forward, surpassing obstacles, has never been made possible by hiding one's fears or weaknesses. But, on the contrary, by accepting them, facing them head on, and eventually relying on them.

Our so-called balances are just a sum of imbalances.

Alone, blinded by the myth of individual omnipotence, we are worth nothing.
The high-wire walker has no power without those who accompany her. Secure her ascent. Rise her lines. Put her into music. Dress her. Watch her. Look after her. Enchant her.
It is no longer a superhuman figure walking on a wire, but a triangular association that goes forward and grows. Camped on the ground and in the air.

Each performance will follow a several day settlement, during which volunteers of all ages will be trained in the practice and the accompaniment of high-wire walking, as well as in the music, choir and songs which will rhythm the phases of the performance.

For this creation and the production of most of its spectacular effects, Basinga launched a series of technical researches which will doubtfully revolutionize the practice of tightrope/ high-wire walking.

Hyphens

The Basinga team proposes courses, seminars and workshops, questionning the art of high-wire walking, and aiming at immersing the participants into the very sensations, emotions and specificities of this discipline : risk taming, self-balance, self-knowledge, concentration and endurance.

Those seminars and workshops – or « high-phens » as we call them – result in the opportunity for participants to take part in a Crossing (small form) or a Tangent (big form) and thus contribute to making these performances truly unique.

Sreening, debate "Place Of Risk"

Cavaletti are ropes, set astride the main wire, used to hold it straight by limiting the left/right swing. For the Crossings as well as for it Great Form (Tangent), Basinga requires 10 to 20 people to hold these cavaletti, sit on them as on a seat, and give weight when the walker passes to his/her level. Lined up to each other, every 10m, the cavalettists form a human guard, essential to the walker's progression.
(1 to several hours depending on the project. Age 18 years minimum. Weight 60 kg minimum. Do not drink alcohol on the day of the show)

Open air screening of James Marsh's documentary "Man on Wire" which was awarded an Oscar for best documentary in 2009. This film focuses on the unusual journey of Philippe Petit, a French artist who illegally installed a wire between the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center and committed the "artistic crime of the century."

At the end of this screening Basinga proposes a public debate with Tatiana-Mosio Bongonga - herself a high-wire walker – together with guests whose research, work or passion stretches the boundaries of commonly accepted risk limitations.

What is risk? What place does it hold in your everyday life? Does overcoming your fears necessarily mean taking a risk ? Who are we protecting when we limit activities involving high risk ?
The debate will allow us an opportunity to reconsider these socially challenging issues so as we might gain a deeper appreciation of the artist upon the wire.

High-wire discovery workshops intended for families. Not only for parents and children, but for grand-parents and their grand-children, brothers and sisters, uncle and nephews… An experience during which the usual stereotypes of family-relationship are challenged, brought to distend – or even reverse – while the bodies and minds confront the difficulties related to height and imbalance. (duration : 3 hours. For anyone who knows how to walk for 7 to 77 and even more)

"When I hold a person on the wire, barely 50 cm from the ground, when I feel his/her heart pounding, and see his/her face breathlessly contorted, I tell myself : I can't leave this person like that, I need to help him/her to overcome his/her fear, in order him/her to realize that this has no reason to be ! It's only a question of adapting oneself. Each unstable situation always encounters a more stable solution (but certainly not stable)!"

Tatiana-Mosio BONGONGA.

Small exercises, and funny games, to discover different balance-challenging circus gears : the ball, the wire, simple ground acrobatics... This workshop aims to play with one's own dexterity in all situations. The session starts with a quick warm-up. It goes on with some game-type proprioception exercises on the ground, alone and in pairs. Once everybody ready, the discovery of the different types of gear begins.
(Duration : 1 hour. For children and teenagers. Children of 8 years and less must be accompanied)
